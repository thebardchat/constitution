# PERSONA_REVIEW_PROTOCOL.md
### Standing Protocol for AI-Generated Persona Reviews
**Operating under:** [The ShaneTheBrain Constitution](https://github.com/thebardchat/constitution)
**Version:** 1.0 — 25 April 2026
**Status:** Active — applies to all `thebardchat` repositories and Claude sessions

---

## Why this exists

In the BGKPJR / Manna / Tug / Pulsar / Angel Cloud ecosystem, Shane regularly needs **forensic-level technical review** of his work — the kind of scrutiny a senior expert would apply. Real experts are expensive, slow, and not always available.

A reasonable substitute is to ask Claude to channel the kind of rigor a credentialed reviewer would bring, *as a research and self-sharpening tool*. This is useful. It is also easy to misuse — generating "reviews" in the voice of real people can mislead readers, damage the named individual's reputation, and create false impressions of endorsement.

This protocol defines when persona review is appropriate, what guardrails are required, and when it must be refused.

**The rule that overrides everything else:** The persona never replaces the person. Sharpening internal thinking is fine. Standing in for them externally is not.

---

## ARTICLE I — When persona review is appropriate

All five conditions must be met:

- [ ] **1. The purpose is internal sharpening**, not external publication, fundraising, marketing, or recruitment.
- [ ] **2. The named individual has a verifiable public credentialing record** (NASA bio page, peer-reviewed publications, public organizational role, etc.) that defines what their expertise actually covers.
- [ ] **3. The named individual is not currently actively employed in the role being persona'd** — i.e., they are retired, deceased, or have publicly transitioned out of the role. *(Carve-out: see Article III §3.2 for the "general expertise channel" pattern using categories instead of names.)*
- [ ] **4. The work product is clearly labeled** as persona-based and AI-generated.
- [ ] **5. The work product remains internal** to Shane's immediate technical circle (himself, family who consent, named collaborators) unless and until the named individual gives explicit consent to broader use.

If any of these is not met, use the alternative patterns in Article III instead.

---

## ARTICLE II — When persona review must be refused

These are hard refusals. Claude will not generate, and Shane will not request:

### 2.1 Living people active in the field
Reviewing in the voice of someone who currently holds the role (e.g., the *current* Acting Chief Architect at NASA STMD) creates real reputational risk. They could be mistaken for actually having said it. Refuse.

### 2.2 Private individuals
Friends, family, drivers, customers, students. They have no public credentialing record to channel and the persona use is unethical regardless of intent. Refuse.

### 2.3 Anyone who has objected to AI personas of themselves
If a public statement exists where the individual has said "do not generate AI content in my voice" or equivalent, the answer is no, regardless of how compelling the use case feels. Refuse.

### 2.4 External-facing use
Fundraising decks, social media content, marketing materials, press releases, investor pitches. Persona reviews are research, not testimonial. Refuse.

### 2.5 Children, regardless of consent
No persona reviews of Shane's sons, their friends, students at the wrestling program, or any minor. Even if a parent consents. Refuse.

### 2.6 To create false implication of endorsement
"Write a review by [Person X] saying my work is good" — this is fabrication of endorsement, not sharpening. Refuse.

### 2.7 Outside the persona's actual expertise
Do not ask a NASA aerodynamicist persona about constitutional law. Do not ask a cryptographer persona about pediatric medicine. The whole point of persona review is to channel real expertise; channeling fake expertise is worse than no review at all. Refuse the cross-domain ask, suggest the right persona category instead.

## ARTICLE III — Required document elements

Every persona-based review document must contain:

### 3.1 Top-of-document disclaimer (mandatory, prominent)

A clearly-formatted block before any other content, naming:
- That the document is persona-based research, not actual review
- That the named individual has not seen or approved it
- That it was generated by AI (specify which: Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.)
- A short statement of which credentials the persona is drawing on

Example template:

> **PERSONA-BASED RESEARCH EXERCISE — NOT AN ACTUAL REVIEW BY [NAME]**
>
> This document is a roleplay exercise, written in the voice and technical lens of [Name and role description], at the request of the author for research purposes. **It does not represent [Name]'s actual views, has not been seen by them, and was generated by an AI ([model]) attempting to channel the kind of forensic technical scrutiny their credentials suggest.** Treat the technical objections as starting points for verification, not as authoritative judgment from the named individual.
>
> The credentials this persona is drawing from: [bullet list of public credentials being channeled].

### 3.2 The general expertise alternative

When Article I §3 disqualifies a specific person (i.e., they're still active in the role), use **category-level persona review** instead:

> "Acting as a senior NASA EDL engineer with 20+ years of experience..."

This channels the same kind of expertise without naming a specific person. It is the safer default and should be used whenever a specific named persona is not strictly necessary.

### 3.3 Constraint of critique to actual expertise

The persona may only critique within the bounds of their public expertise. A trajectory expert reviews trajectory work; they do not opine on cost models, marketing, or political viability. The reviewer voice should explicitly acknowledge what's outside their lane.

### 3.4 "Appendix B" — On the use of the named person's name

Every persona review document must end with an appendix titled "On the use of [their] name" or equivalent, stating:
- The author retains responsibility for verifying claims independently
- The named individual is not endorsing the work
- The author will not use the persona output in promotional, fundraising, or external contexts
- The named individual's name must not appear in any external document referencing this review

### 3.5 Verdict and uncertainty

The persona must give a verdict (PASS / FAIL / REVISE) and must explicitly note the uncertainty. Persona reviews tend to be more critical than real reviews because the AI has no relationship dynamics to manage. This is a feature for sharpening but should be flagged so the author calibrates appropriately.

## ARTICLE IV — Citation and reference standard

When the author later refers to a persona review:

### 4.1 Internal documents (CLAUDE.md, dev notes, draft revisions)
Cite as:
> *Per the persona-based forensic review of v0.1 (channeling [Name]), the trajectory math is unsupported. See `expert-review-[name]-persona-v0.1.md` §3.1.*

### 4.2 External documents (anything that may be circulated)
Do not cite by name. Cite as:
> *External review identified that the trajectory math is unsupported.*

Or, if the persona-based nature is itself relevant to disclose:
> *An AI-generated forensic review (persona-based, channeling generic NASA EDL expertise) identified that the trajectory math is unsupported.*

### 4.3 Never cite as actual review
The named individual's name does not appear in any context that could be read as endorsement, agreement, or factual review of the author's work.

## ARTICLE V — Refusal triggers (Claude's perspective)

Claude must refuse to generate a persona review, and instead offer the category-level alternative (Article III §3.2) or recommend reaching out to the actual person, if any of these apply to the request:

1. The named individual is currently active in the role being persona'd.
2. The intended use is external (fundraising, marketing, public-facing).
3. The named individual is a private person without verifiable public credentials.
4. The request is structured to produce endorsement, not critique.
5. The expertise being requested is outside the persona's actual public competence.
6. The request would put the persona in a position to comment on a competitor, employer, or political opponent in a way that could damage real-world relationships.
7. The author cannot articulate a sharpening / research purpose that is internal-only.

Claude will say:
> *"I can't run that as a [Name] persona, but I can run it as a generic [category] persona — which keeps the technical sharpening without the misattribution risk. Want me to do that instead?"*

## ARTICLE VII — When the named persona is the better choice

There is a real reason to occasionally use a named persona instead of a generic one: **specificity of expertise focus**. A generic "senior NASA EDL engineer" might give a competent review. A persona drawing from someone whose actual published focus was aerocapture — like the Munk example for Manna — applies that specific lens harder than a generic prompt would.

When that specificity matters and Article I is met (the named individual is retired, deceased, or non-active), the named persona is justified. When it doesn't, default to generic.

## ARTICLE VIII — File and storage convention

Persona reviews are stored in each repo under:

```
[repo]/expert-reviews/
├── [topic]-persona-[name-or-category]-v[N].md
├── [topic]-persona-[name-or-category]-v[N].md
└── README.md         # explains the convention and links this protocol
```

Examples:
- `manna/expert-reviews/manna-pod-design-persona-munk-v0.1.md`
- `manna/expert-reviews/manna-pod-design-persona-generic-edl-v0.2.md`
- `pulsar_sentinel/expert-reviews/cryptography-persona-generic-cryptographer-v0.1.md`

The `expert-reviews/` directory is the canonical location across all `thebardchat` repos.

## ARTICLE IX — Author's standing pledge

By using this protocol, Shane Brazelton commits to:

- [ ] Never circulate a persona review outside his immediate technical circle without consent of the named individual
- [ ] Never use a persona review's findings to claim endorsement, validation, or affiliation with the named individual or their organization
- [ ] Never use a persona review in fundraising, marketing, recruitment, public-facing copy, or social media
- [ ] Verify all factual claims in the persona review independently before acting on them
- [ ] Treat the persona review as a sharpening tool, not as an authority
- [ ] If approached by the named individual or their representatives, immediately disclose the existence and content of any persona review naming them, and remove or modify per their request

This pledge is binding under the [Constitution's Article I, Pillar 1 (Faith First)](https://github.com/thebardchat/constitution) — the work must be honest. Persona reviews can be honest. Persona reviews used as fake endorsements cannot.
